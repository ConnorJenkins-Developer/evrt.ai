Founder’s Note

EVRT Execution First
Connor Jenkins
Founder, EVRT
Consulting Data Analysis Marketing Software Development

I started EVRT because too many businesses are stuck between strategy and execution. They have data, but no visibility. They have tools, but no system. They have ideas, but no infrastructure to turn those ideas into repeatable outcomes.

My background sits at the intersection of data analysis, marketing, and software development. That combination matters because most business problems are not isolated. Revenue issues usually touch the website, the funnel, the reporting, the workflow, and the decision-making process at the same time.

EVRT was built to close that gap. Not with abstract recommendations, but with working systems: dashboards that surface what matters, websites that convert better, automation that removes wasted motion, and implementation that ties the work back to actual business performance.

Operating Principles

How we think and work
1) Clarity before complexity

If the business cannot clearly see what is happening, more tools will not fix it. We simplify before we scale.

2) Systems over guesswork

Strong businesses do not rely on memory, intuition, or scattered notes. They rely on processes, structure, and visibility.

3) Execution matters more than presentation

Decks do not create leverage. Working systems do. Our bias is always toward implementation.

4) Measure what matters

Vanity metrics create noise. We focus on the numbers that affect revenue, conversion, efficiency, and decision quality.

5) Build for compounding value

Each improvement should make the business easier to run, easier to understand, and easier to grow over time.

6) Make the next move obvious

Good systems reduce hesitation. The best output is not more information. It is better decisions.
